# Finance Support Analyst Brief

## Overview

This brief describes a finance support role in a large, complex public-sector organisation. The team supports budget holders, helps resolve transaction queries, analyses budget variance, and prepares standard financial reporting.

The work is cross-functional and sits close to the month-end and budget-holder support cycle.

## Core Responsibilities

- Provide help and support to budget holders on budget variance analysis and complex queries.
- Act as the main day-to-day contact for transaction-related questions.
- Support management accounts and finance support staff with analysis of complex data and preparation of standard monthly reports.
- Respond to ad hoc requests from budget holders and finance business partners for reports and financial information.

## Skills and Experience

- Financial management experience in a medium to large multi-disciplined organisation.
- Strong knowledge of general ledger systems.
- Good understanding of accounting standards, finance legislation, and governance.
- Strong Microsoft Office skills, especially Excel and Access.

## Qualifications and Development

- Degree level education or equivalent experience.
- Commitment to working towards a full professional accountancy qualification.
- Commitment to ongoing professional development.

## Automation-Friendly Work

The most automation-friendly parts of the role are the ones that repeat every day or every month:

- Query triage and routing
- First-pass responses to budget-holder questions
- Variance commentary drafting
- Standard monthly report assembly
- Ad hoc report generation from recurring templates
- Flagging missing information or unusual transactions

## What AI Can Help With

AI can speed up the repetitive front end of the role by:

- classifying incoming requests
- drafting responses from structured finance data
- suggesting likely explanations for variances
- turning spreadsheet inputs into readable summaries
- generating consistent monthly commentary
- identifying exceptions for human review

The human analyst still owns judgement, approval, escalation, and stakeholder management.
